LEGACY OF HEROES

Honoring individuals whose courage, sacrifice, service, leadership, and commitment continue to inspire future generations.

Why We Remember

Legacy is not measured by fame or fortune. It is measured by the lives improved, protected, inspired, and forever changed through service to something greater than oneself.

Military Heroes

🏅 Audie Murphy

The most decorated American combat soldier of World War II. His extraordinary courage under fire near Holtzwihr, France became one of the defining acts of battlefield heroism in military history.

Learn More

🏅 Roy Benavidez

A Green Beret who repeatedly exposed himself to enemy fire during a six-hour battle in Vietnam, saving lives despite suffering dozens of wounds and refusing to quit.

Learn More

🏅 Charles Whittlesey

Commander of the legendary Lost Battalion during World War I. Surrounded, outnumbered, and under constant attack, he refused surrender and held the line until rescue arrived.

Learn More

First Responders

🏅 Stephen Siller

An off-duty FDNY firefighter who ran through the Brooklyn Battery Tunnel on September 11, 2001 carrying sixty pounds of gear in order to help others at the World Trade Center.

Learn More

🏅 Jason Brezler

Marine combat leader and FDNY firefighter whose work continues to improve the mental preparedness and resilience of first responders operating in high-stress environments.

Learn More

True Patriots

🏅 Dr. Charles Drew

Pioneer of modern blood plasma preservation whose innovations saved countless lives during World War II and whose personal principles reflected a deep commitment to human dignity.

Learn More

🏅 Katherine Johnson

NASA mathematician whose calculations helped make early American spaceflight possible and whose excellence overcame barriers that once seemed impossible to break.

Learn More

Everyday Heroes

🏅 James Harrison

Known as "The Man with the Golden Arm," his blood donations over several decades helped save the lives of more than two million babies around the world.
